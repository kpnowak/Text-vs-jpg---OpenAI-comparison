### Review of Document: How to talk about treatment options

#### Overall Assessment:
The document portrays itself as an educational resource for patients with type 2 diabetes, providing advice on discussing treatment options with healthcare professionals. We will review the content based on the "Non-Promotional Material Guidelines."

#### Detailed Comments and Corrections:

1. **Source Sentence:**
   - "When you’re living with type 2 diabetes, it’s common to reevaluate and change your treatment to adjust to your changing personal health needs."

   **Deviations:**
   - This statement can be perceived as indirect promotion of treatment changes.
   - According to **[Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 16]**, the content must be "fair, balanced and objective."

   **New Sentence:**
   - "When living with type 2 diabetes, it is essential to continually monitor your condition and discuss any necessary treatment adjustments with your healthcare provider."

   **Reason for Replacement:**
   - The modification ensures the sentence remains an objective encouragement of dialogue rather than suggesting specific changes to treatment.
   
2. **Source Sentence:**
   - "Your doctor is the medical expert, but you are the one living with type 2 diabetes. You know your mind and body, and it’s important to advocate for your needs and preferences during your discussion."

   **Deviations:**
   - The tone could be perceived as undermining the authority of healthcare providers.
   - According to **[Source - Communicate With Care Pocket Guide, Article 2]**, content should have a professional tone and avoid any statements that could be interpreted as undermining professional advice.

   **New Sentence:**
   - "While your doctor is the medical expert, your input about your daily experiences and preferences is valuable in making informed treatment decisions."

   **Reason for Replacement:**
   - The sentence now respects the professional authority of HCPs while emphasizing the importance of patient input.

3. **Source Sentence:**
   - "Talk about your lifestyle and how a new treatment might be better suited for you."

   **Deviations:**
   - This sentence might appear to advocate for changing treatments without professional input.
   - According to **[Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17]**, materials must not serve as an inducement to change treatment.

   **New Sentence:**
   - "Discuss your lifestyle with your doctor to ensure your current treatment aligns with your daily routines and preferences."

   **Reason for Replacement:**
   - The sentence ensures conversation about aligning treatment with lifestyle without suggesting a change in treatment.

4. **Source Sentence:**
   - "Ask questions or share concerns you have about trying a new medication."

   **Deviations:**
   - The statement may indirectly suggest changing to a new medication.
   - According to **[Source - EFPIA Chapter 4, Article 21]**, communications should not prompt patients to seek new medications.

   **New Sentence:**
   - "Share any concerns or questions you have regarding your current medication and its effects."

   **Reason for Replacement:**
   - Focuses on concerns with current medication, avoiding prompts for new medications.
   
5. **Source Sentence:**
   - "Typically, type 2 diabetes patients should see their doctor every 3-6 months."

   **Deviations:**
   - This might be seen as providing medical advice.
   - According to **[Source - Disclaimer Texts for Non-promotional Material, Article 3, General Disclaimers]**, medical advice should be left to healthcare professionals.

   **New Sentence:**
   - "It is important to follow your doctor's recommendations on the frequency of follow-up visits."

   **Reason for Replacement:**
   - Keeps advice in line with recommendations from healthcare providers without suggesting specific intervals.

6. **Source Inclusion:**
   - **Disclaimer Text:** The document lacks a disclaimer that clarifies the educational purpose of the content and that it should not substitute for professional medical advice.

   **New Sentence:**
   - "This information is intended for educational purposes only and should not be substituted for medical advice from your doctor. Please see the patient information leaflet for additional details."

   **Reason for Inclusion:**
   - Important to ensure there is no confusion about the document’s purpose.

### Conclusion
By making the adjustments and ensuring adherence to the guidelines, the document can effectively serve its purpose of educating patients without veering into the realm of indirect promotion or providing explicit medical advice. Each change ensures clarity, professionalism, and alignment with ethical standards.
