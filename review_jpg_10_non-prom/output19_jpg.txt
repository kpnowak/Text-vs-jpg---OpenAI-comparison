### Review and Corrections Based on Non-Promotional Material Guidelines

#### Main Text and Headings Review

1. **Original Source Sentence:**
   "When you’re living with type 2 diabetes, it’s common to reevaluate and change your treatment to adjust to your changing personal health needs."

   **Deviation from Expert Reviewer:**
   The source sentence could be interpreted ambiguously regarding what specific actions the patient should take, which could be perceived as promotional.

   **Expert Reviewer Suggested Change:**
   "When managing type 2 diabetes, it’s important to regularly assess your treatment plan in collaboration with your healthcare provider to ensure it meets your changing health needs."

   **Explanation for New Suggested Change:**
   The expert's suggestion improves on the original but can further emphasize the non-promotional aspect by eliminating any implication of self-medication.

   **New Suggested Sentence:**
   "Managing type 2 diabetes involves regularly reviewing and adjusting your treatment plan with your healthcare provider to meet your evolving health needs."

   **Deviations:**
   According to [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17.ii], Informational or Educational Materials must directly benefit the care of patients without suggesting self-treatment that could be perceived as promotional.
