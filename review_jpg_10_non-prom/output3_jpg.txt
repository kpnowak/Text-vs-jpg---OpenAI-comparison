### Section 2: Goals

“Share your personal goals, whether it’s better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing the risk of cardiovascular events, and discuss with your doctor how your current treatment could be more effective.”

**Deviation:**

**Expert Reviewer’s Suggested Change:**
"Discuss your personal goals such as improving blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing cardiovascular risks, and review with your doctor whether your current treatment meets these objectives."

**Reason for Your New Suggested Change:**

**Your New Suggested Sentence:**
"Communicate your personal health goals like improving blood sugar control, managing weight, or reducing cardiovascular risks, and assess with your doctor how well your current treatment aligns with these aims."

**Deviation Explanation:**
According to [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 16], guidelines emphasize acknowledging the role of current treatment without promoting changes.

### Section 3: Challenges
**Original Source Sentence:**
“Discuss any difficulties you’ve experienced with your current treatment—such as side effects, if you would prefer a pill or an injection (if the option is available), if it fits into your lifestyle, if the cost is too high—and ask if there might find be a better alternative that addresses these challenges.”

**Deviation:**
The sentence introduces options such as “a pill or an injection” and discusses cost which might seem promotional.

**Expert Reviewer’s Suggested Change:**
"Discuss any difficulties you're experiencing with your current treatment, including side effects and how well it fits into your lifestyle, and ask about possible ways to address these challenges."

**Reason for Your New Suggested Change:**
The suggestion is good but should also mitigate perceived promotion by solely focusing on existing treatment concerns.

**Your New Suggested Sentence:**
"Talk about any challenges you face with your current treatment, such as side effects or how it fits into your lifestyle, and inquire about addressing these challenges alongside your doctor."

**Deviation Explanation:**
According to [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17], informational or educational content must not suggest product choices.

### Section 4: Lifestyle
**Original Source Sentence:**
“Talk about your lifestyle and how a new treatment might be better suited for you. For example, if you travel a lot for work and are looking for a medication that is easy to take or maybe you don’t have time to exercise as much as you would like, and you would prefer a treatment that can support your weight management goals (in addition to healthy eating and exercise).”

**Deviation:**
The sentence suggests preference for new treatment options, which can be perceived as promotional.

**Expert Reviewer’s Suggested Change:**
"Discuss your lifestyle factors, such as the need for convenience in managing your medication, and strategies to support your weight management goals together with healthy eating and exercise."

**Reason for Your New Suggested Change:**
While the expert's suggestion is mostly adequate, clarifying lifestyle influence without implying product change is essential.

**Your New Suggested Sentence:**
"Talk about your lifestyle, highlighting needs such as medication convenience, and discuss strategies for support in weight management, incorporating healthy eating and exercise."

**Deviation Explanation:**
According to [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17], educational material should refrain from implying specific treatments.
