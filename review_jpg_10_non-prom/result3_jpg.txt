### Review and Corrections Based on the "Non-Promotional Material Guidelines"

#### 1. Analysis of "Share your personal goals, whether it’s better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing the risk of cardiovascular events, and discuss with your doctor how your current treatment could be more effective."
- **Guideline Source**: EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 16, ii.a
- **Source Sentence**: "Share your personal goals, whether it’s better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing the risk of cardiovascular events, and discuss with your doctor how your current treatment could be more effective."
- **Deviations**: The sentence should reflect balanced scientific content acceptable under the guidelines.
    - **According to EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 16, LLH activities must have content that is fair, balanced, and objective, designed to allow the expression of diverse evidence-based science.**
- **New Sentence**: "Share your personal goals related to better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing cardiovascular risk, and discuss with your doctor how evidence-based adjustments to your treatment plan could enhance effectiveness."
- **Reasoning**: By including "evidence-based adjustments," the sentence reinforces objectivity and aligns with the scientific-based requirement from the guidelines. 

#### 2. Analysis of "Discuss any difficulties you’ve experienced with your current treatment—such as side effects, if you would prefer a pill or an injection (if the option is available), if it fits into your lifestyle, if the cost is too high—and ask if there might find a better alternative that addresses these challenges."
- **Guideline Source**: EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17, ii.
- **Source Sentence**: "Discuss any difficulties you’ve experienced with your current treatment—such as side effects, if you would prefer a pill or an injection (if the option is available), if it fits into your lifestyle, if the cost is too high—and ask if there might be a better alternative that addresses these challenges."
- **Deviations**: The sentence must ensure any advice does not offset routine business practices or constitute promotion.
    - **According to EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17, education items must not circumvent the prohibition on gifts and promotions.**
- **New Sentence**: "Discuss any challenges experienced with your current treatment, including side effects, preference for pill or injection, compatibility with your lifestyle, and cost considerations. Seek evidence-based advice on alternative treatments that could better address these issues."
- **Reasoning**: This ensures the content is aligned with non-promotional, educational guidance, focusing on evidence-based advice.

#### 3. Analysis of "Talk about your lifestyle and how a new treatment might be better suited for you."
- **Guideline Source**: EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17, iv.
- **Source Sentence**: "Talk about your lifestyle and how a new treatment might be better suited for you."
- **Deviations**: Should not use language perceived as promoting specific products.
    - **According to EFPIA Chapter 3, Article 17, iv, materials must not be product branded unless essential for the correct use.**

- **Reasoning**: Refocusing on treatment options in general maintains objectivity and avoids product-specific promotion.
