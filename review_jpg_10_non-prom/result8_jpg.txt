**Review of Promotional Material**

### 1. Sentence: "It’s important that you feel empowered and ready to talk to your doctor whenever you feel a change could be beneficial."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - Communicate With Care Pocket Guide: Article 1 - Be Mindful, Article 2 - Be Clear and Professional]

**Source Sentence**: "It’s important that you feel empowered and ready to talk to your doctor whenever you feel a change could be beneficial."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence implies a broad empowerment which may be construed as promotional.
2. It does not provide clarity on how to prepare for the conversation.
3. It lacks the necessary professional context.

**New Sentence**:
"To discuss potential changes in treatment, ensure you are informed about your health situation and prepare specific questions to ask your doctor for an effective conversation."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This sentence maintains a professional tone, focusing on informed and prepared patient interactions without implying promotional encouragement. This approach aligns with Article 2 of the Communicate With Care Pocket Guide to ensure clarity and context while avoiding exaggeration.

---

### 2. Sentence: "Share your personal goals, whether it’s better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing the risk of cardiovascular events."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3: Article 17 - Informational or Educational Materials and Items of Medical Utility]

**Source Sentence**: "Share your personal goals, whether it’s better blood sugar control, weight management, or reducing the risk of cardiovascular events."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence encourages discussion of personal goals which could imply commercial interest by focusing on specific health improvements.
2. It may lack the necessary professional and non-promotional context.

**New Sentence**:
"Discuss your health priorities with your doctor, such as maintaining appropriate blood sugar levels, managing weight, or addressing cardiovascular risk."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This sentence focuses on general health priorities rather than suggesting specific goals. It maintains a non-promotional, informational stance in line with Article 17, which emphasizes informative material without inducing specific product use.

---

### 3. Sentence: "Talk about how your current treatment could be more effective."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3: Article 16 - Lifelong Learning in Healthcare]

**Source Sentence**: "Talk about how your current treatment could be more effective."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence simplifies treatment effectiveness discussion, thus risking an oversimplified view of medical efficacy.
2. It lacks balanced scientific context.

**New Sentence**:
"Discuss evidence-based ways to possibly enhance the effectiveness of your current treatment with your healthcare provider."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This revision emphasizes discussing evidence-based improvements, promoting a balanced science-based dialogue that adheres to lifelong learning principles while avoiding promotional content.

---

### 4. Sentence: "Discuss any difficulties you’ve experienced with your current treatment—such as side effects."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - EFPIA Chapter 3: Article 17 - Informational or Educational Materials and Items of Medical Utility]

**Source Sentence**: "Discuss any difficulties you’ve experienced with your current treatment—such as side effects."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence focuses extensively on difficulties, which could implicitly discourage use of current treatments.
2. It requires a broader perspective on patient-provider discussion.

**New Sentence**:
"Share any challenges you face with your current treatment with your healthcare provider, including side effects or other concerns."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This sentence broadens the discussion to include any challenges rather than focusing solely on difficulties, promoting balanced and informative interaction consistent with Article 17 guidelines.

---

### 5. Sentence: "Remember, this conversation is about working together with your doctor to find the best treatment option for you."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - EFPIA Chapter 4: Article 21 - Interactions with POs]

**Source Sentence**: "Remember, this conversation is about working together with your doctor to find the best treatment option for you."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence implies subjective judgment in finding the "best" treatment, which might indicate favoritism.
2. It should emphasize evidence-based and patient-specific decisions.

**New Sentence**:
"Remember, the goal of this conversation is to collaborate with your healthcare provider to determine the most appropriate treatment based on your specific health needs."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This revision emphasizes collaboration and specificity, ensuring discussions are evidence-based and patient-specific. It maintains neutrality and avoids suggesting any treatment preference aligned with Article 21 principles.

---

### 6. Sentence: "Discuss how you should follow-up on how your new treatment is working—typically, type 2 diabetes patients should see their doctor every 3–6 months."

**Guideline Source**: [Source - Disclaimer Texts for Non-promotional Material: Article 3 - General Disclaimers]

**Source Sentence**: "Discuss how you should follow-up on how your new treatment is working—typically, type 2 diabetes patients should see their doctor every 3–6 months."

**Deviations and Corrections**:
1. The sentence provides a specific follow-up period, which might not apply universally.
2. It should include a disclaimer to refer to personal healthcare advice.

**New Sentence**:
"Discuss with your healthcare provider the appropriate follow-up schedule to monitor how your new treatment is working, keeping in mind that individual needs may vary."

**Reason for Replacement**:
This revision advises a customized follow-up schedule discussion, adding flexibility and including a reference to individual health needs, which aligns with providing general disclaimers to avoid prescriptive statements.
